Polonnaruwa District is one of the 25 districts of Sri Lanka, the second level administrative division of the country. It is also one of the two districts of North Central Province and has an area of 3,293 kmÂ².

The district is administered by a District Secretariat headed by a District Secretary (previously known as a Government Agent) appointed by the central government of Sri Lanka. The capital of the district is the city of Polonnaruwa.

Minneriya

ancient Minneriya rainwater reservoir encompassed in the Minneriya National Park is home to hoards of elephants. Among the other mammals in the park are sambar, spotted deer, sloth bears and leopards.Minneriya National Park forms an area of elephant corridor connecting Kaudulla National Park and Wasgomuwa National Park. During July to October, large hoards of elephants gather at Minneriya reservoir when water reservoirs in the surrounding areas have dried up.



Giritale

Giritale is located 199 km away from Colombo in the North-central province of Sri Lanka.Giritale, 12 km North-west of Polonnaruwa, can be reached from main motor roads from Colombo that run via Dambulla, Habarana and Minneriya. Giritale is not connected by a railway line. The nearest station is at Kaduruwela, Polonnaruwa.

Giritale that sit pretty on the banks of Giritale man-made reservoir with Minneriya Sanctuary centered around the Minneriya reservoir, located at a distance of 11 km.Giritale, a nature and birdlife destination with beautiful Giritale Reservoir that set up the focal point, is the preferred accommodation option of those who visit the World Heritage Site of Polonnaruwa.
